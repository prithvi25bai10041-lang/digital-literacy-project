# Cybercrime Case Study: UPI Payment Fraud

UPI payment fraud has become one of the most widespread cybercrimes in India,
especially among college students who frequently use digital payment applications. 
This type of fraud typically involves deception, where the attacker manipulates 
the victim into performing an action that results in unauthorized transfer of money.

In a common scenario, a student posts an item for sale online. The scammer contacts 
the student pretending to be a genuine buyer and agrees to purchase the item. Instead 
of making a direct payment, the scammer sends a QR code or a payment request and 
instructs the student to scan it to “receive” the money. Trusting the buyer, the 
student scans the code and unknowingly authorizes a payment from their own account.

In another variation, the attacker impersonates a bank official or customer care 
representative and informs the victim about an issue with their account. The victim 
is then asked to share sensitive information such as OTPs (One-Time Passwords), debit
card details, or login credentials. Once the attacker obtains this information, they 
gain access to the victim’s account and transfer money without permission.

Students and young users are particularly vulnerable due to their frequent use of UPI 
apps like Google Pay, PhonePe, and Paytm, combined with a lack of awareness about such 
scams. The consequences can include immediate financial loss, emotional stress, and 
potential misuse of personal data.

This case study highlights the importance of digital literacy and awareness. Users must understand that receiving money never requires scanning a QR code or sharing OTPs, and any such request should be treated as suspicious.
